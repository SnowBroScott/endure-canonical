## Event Frequency & Cadence (Draft)

Champion Trials and World Events follow an **intentional rhythm** designed to build community and shared memory without creating pressure, obligation, or fear of missing out.

All events are opt‑in. Declining participation is always neutral.

---

### Hold‑Level Champion Trials

- Occur **approximately once a month**, irregular rather than scheduled

- Personal in scope and primarily affect a single hold

- Failure is mostly felt locally; success is celebrated by all

- 1–2 other champions may assist

These events strengthen attachment to a place and its people without becoming routine or mandatory.

---

### Continent‑Level Challenges

- Occur **approximately once per quarter**

- Regional in scope, affecting multiple holds

- Stakes and outcomes are **equally shared** by all participants

- Typically involve 5–10 champions standing together

These events function as seasonal moments of solidarity and shared responsibility.

---

### World‑Level Events

- Occur **approximately once per year**

- Endure‑wide in scope, open to the entire player population

- Participation is always optional and time‑generous

- Outcomes are symbolic, commemorative, and historical

World events are cultural moments that build shared myth, not progression.

---

### Cadence Design Constraints

- Events must never escalate pressure or accumulate penalties

- Missing an event must never create disadvantage

- Participation should feel meaningful, not expected

- If an event would feel bad to ignore, it is too frequent

Events exist to deepen the world, not to demand attention.

## Identity, Expression, and Memory (Player‑Facing) (Draft)

### Who You Are vs. What You’ve Lived

In Endure, there are two different ways your character can change — and they mean different things.

Some parts of your appearance are **yours by choice**. 

Your hairstyle, clothing, body presentation, and personal style exist so you can decide who you are *before* the world responds to you. These choices are always free, always reversible, and never judged. They don’t affect outcomes, performance, or progression.

Other changes are **left behind by experience**. 

Marks like tattoos, scars, totems, or ritual symbols aren’t chosen at a menu — they’re earned through moments that mattered. They exist because something happened. You stood for something. The world remembers that.

---

### Expression Is Choice. Marks Are Memory.

You’ll always be free to express yourself. 

You’ll never be required to earn the right to look like yourself.

Some moments leave traces. Those traces aren’t upgrades or status symbols. They don’t make you better than anyone else — they simply say *this is part of your story*. Even if your path changes later, those marks remain as truth, not obligation.

In Endure, nothing asks you to optimize who you are. 

You decide how you look. 

The world decides what it remembers.
