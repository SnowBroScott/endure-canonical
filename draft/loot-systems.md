# Endure – Loot Systems (Draft)

Notebook: Endure
Status: Draft — Working Foundation (Not Locked)

---

## Design Intent

Loot in Endure exists to acknowledge, contextualize, and add
meaning to endurance — not to replace or optimize it.

- XP measures that effort occurred.
- Loot reflects how effort was endured.
- Transformation reflects what the player becomes over time.

Loot is intentionally separated from XP and long-term
progression. Its role is experiential, expressive, and
interpretive — not mechanical acceleration.

---

## The Four Loot Categories

Loot in Endure falls into three main categories plus one
additive category. All four must be named consistently
throughout this document and all downstream documents.

**Blueprints** — Single-use construction permits for the Hold.
Consumed in the act of building. The structure built stands
permanently. The blueprint is gone. To build another structure
of the same type, another blueprint must drop.

No stockpiling. One copy of a blueprint type in inventory
at a time.

Path-neutral at common. Path-specific at uncommon and above.
Ten pools — one per identity across four base classes and six
hybrids. One identity, one pool. Access requires current
behavioral alignment with that identity.

When hybrid status is confirmed, access to base class blueprint
pools ends. One identity, one pool — no exceptions at the
hybrid level.

Blueprints already built are permanent regardless of identity
drift. A player who drifts retains all structures built during
that phase. Eligibility governs future drops only — not
existing construction.

Rarity affects blueprints in three ways simultaneously:
- Drop frequency — rarer blueprints drop less often
- Emotional weight — a mythic blueprint drop is a genuine event
- Structure quality and uniqueness — commons produce functional
  but humble structures. Higher rarities produce more elaborate
  versions of the same structure type. Some structures exist
  only at certain rarity thresholds — a lower rarity version
  of that structure does not exist.

**Aesthetics** — Character expression. Free and reversible.
No resource cost. Path-specific in visual language.
Freely changeable, consequence-free.

**Environment** — Free expressive layer. Weather, terrain,
animals, scenes. No mechanical weight. Does not feed the
weighted identity scale. Freely changeable, consequence-free.

**Consumables** — Fourth distinct category. Additive drops.
Appear alongside normal loot, not part of the main pool.
Invitations toward a non-primary path. Never assignments.
See Consumables section below.

Every drop is genuine loot. There is no junk category.
Desirability is determined by the player in context — loot
the player chooses not to keep converts to resources.
Destroying loot is authorship, not failure.

Marks are not a loot category. They are consequences of
trials and events and belong to a separate system.

## Rarity

Rarity in Endure expresses two things simultaneously:
frequency of occurrence and emotional weight on resolution.
These are not separate systems — they are the same design
decision expressed at two levels.

Rarity does not express power, efficiency, or progression.

Five tiers:
- Common — OK, useful. Expected.
- Uncommon — Cool. A small win.
- Rare — Sweet. Genuinely exciting.
- Epic — Holy shit.
- Mythic — Holy shit, but more so.

Consumables are excluded from the standard rarity system.
They are governed by identity-aware logic.

## The Loot Loop

### During Activity — Mid-Session Drops

During longer efforts only, at irregular and quiet intervals,
players may receive loot. Low probability of being meaningful.
Must never interrupt effort or create anticipation that
distracts from the activity.

Drop profile: commons weighted heavily across all three main
categories. Most will be converted to resources by player
choice. That is their purpose.

### End of Activity — Primary Drop

Each activity concludes with a clear ritual moment where
primary loot is resolved. The floor is uncommon — no commons
at session completion. Rares weighted. Epics and Mythics
possible.

Completing always moves the player up the quality curve.
The player anticipates the moment of resolution, not the
outcome. Emotional punctuation. Closure.

### Post-Activity Resolution

After loot is awarded, players review items and choose how
to resolve each one:

- Use the loot
- Destroy the loot for resources

Both choices are valid, dignified, and productive.
Destroying loot is not failure — it is authorship.
The loot was real. The player chose not to keep it.

---

## Milestone vs. Completion Drops

**Milestone drops:** Commons weighted heavily across all
three main categories. Uncommons possible. Rares at very
low probability. Nothing above rare at milestones.
Resources result from loot resolution — they are not
dropped directly.

**Completion drops:** Uncommon floor — no commons.
Rares weighted. Epics and Mythics possible. Completing
always moves the player up the quality curve.

---

## Resources

Resources are the residual value of endurance. They flow
entirely through the loot pipeline. There are no direct
resource drops.

Loot destroyed by choice or natural dissolution converts
to resources. Resources always have value.

Resources are spent almost exclusively within the
survival/build layer. Two named sinks:
- Hold Construction (primary sink)
- Earned Marks (only non-build sink)

Resources do not affect XP, activity difficulty, loot
probability, transformation speed, or progression.

Consumables that dissolves passively converts to resources over
time. This is loot resolution, not identity change.

## Consumables

Consumables are additive drops alongside normal loot.
They are invitations toward a non-primary path.
Never assignments.

**Slot architecture:**
Three slots — one per non-primary path. One consumable
per path maximum. Slots unlock when primary path is
confirmed. No slots exist during the Traveler phase.

**Lifecycle — three resolution outcomes:**
- Use it: follow the nudge, experience the invitation
- Destroy it: immediate resource conversion
- Do nothing: automatic resource conversion at end
  of lifecycle

All three outcomes have value. No outcome is punishing.
Consumables are the only loot type with a built-in
time dimension.

**Acting on a consumable nudge — two reward outcomes:**
- Guaranteed: resource bonus
- Chance: an aesthetic drop from the nudge path

Consumables do not produce blueprint drops under any
circumstance. Hold construction access is strictly
identity-gated.

**Consumables and session texture:**
Consumables affect session texture — the atmosphere and
character of a session — not its mechanical output.
XP, scale points, and progression are unaffected.

Consumable hooks:
- Temporary and session-bound
- Do not modify XP
- Do not stack or persist
- Influence session texture, not progress rate

---

## Absence and Identity

Absence does not move the behavioral scale. Absence does
not soften identity expression. The scale is
activity-triggered only. Elapsed time has no effect on
existing entries.

The Hold responds to return, not to absence. Nothing
diminishes during absence. The fire goes low, not out.
The world waits.

---

## Design Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)

- Loot must never replace effort with rewards
- Loot must never create mandatory play patterns
- Loot must resist min-max optimization
- Ignoring or rejecting loot must remain valid
  and respected
- Consumables must remain invitations, never assignments
- Loot may reference identity and experience only
- Loot must never reference progress, power,
  or efficiency
- Loot must preserve agency, dignity, and long-term trust
