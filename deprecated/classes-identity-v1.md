# Endure – Classes & Identity (Locked)

## Design Intent

Classes in Endure represent paths of endurance, not rigid roles. They are starting identities shaped by how a player prefers to exert effort and evolve organically based on sustained behavior over time.

The fantasy lens is Norse mysticism: endurance as fate, effort as devotion, mastery as restraint.

Classes exist to:

- Shape early experience without restricting choice

- Bias activities, rewards, and progression

- Provide mythic identity grounded in real effort

- Enable hybrid paths through lived behavior, not respecs

---

## Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

1. Classes bias, they do not restrict 

   Classes influence probability and emphasis, never access.

2. Behavior outweighs declaration 

   Long-term activity patterns matter more than initial choice.

3. Hybridization is earned 

   Identity evolves through consistent cross-discipline effort.

4. Identity must be felt 

   A player should feel their class through play, not menus.

---

## Initial Class Selection (Onboarding)

At onboarding, the player selects a Primary Class aligned to the type of physical effort they most prefer.

This choice:

- Establishes initial mythic identity

- Biases surfaced activities

- Influences early stat tendencies

- Shapes early loot probabilities

This choice is not permanent.

---

## Core Class Paths

### Strider

**Lane:** Sustained cyclic movement 

**Primary Work:** Running, intervals, distance, pacing 

**Essence:** The Long Road

Striders endure through motion. Distance, breath, and rhythm define their path. They favor smooth, sustained effort and efficiency over time.

---

### Forgeborn

**Lane:** Strength under resistance 

**Primary Work:** Lifting, loaded reps, controlled force 

**Essence:** Iron and Fire

Forgeborn are shaped by resistance. Strength is earned under pressure, through deliberate load and control. Power is tempered, not given.

---

### Unbroken

**Lane:** High-intensity bodyweight and impact endurance 

**Primary Work:** Plyometrics, explosive effort, discomfort under repetition 

**Essence:** Attrition Without Yield

Unbroken thrive in intensity and impact. They endure repeated strain, explosive output, and discomfort without external load. Their strength is proven through relentless effort.

**Aligned Activities Include:**

- Plyometrics and jumping

- Burpees, box jumps, tuck jumps

- Sprint intervals

- Agility drills

- High-rep bodyweight circuits

- Dynamic lunges and step-ups

- Short-rest, high-intensity conditioning

Clear distinction:

- Strider moves long and smooth

- Forgeborn moves heavy and deliberate

- Unbroken moves fast, brutal, and relentless

---

### Adept

**Lane:** Body mastery and precision 

**Primary Work:** Mobility, flexibility, balance, controlled bodyweight, isometrics 

**Essence:** Self-Mastery

Adepts train control, awareness, and range of motion as primary disciplines. Their strength lies in precision, stability, and intentional movement.

Adept is not recovery. 

It is the training of control as a first-class path.

**Aligned Activities Include:**

- Mobility and flexibility flows

- Balance and stability work

- Controlled bodyweight strength

- Isometric holds

- Tempo-focused movement

- Joint control and alignment

- Breath-guided movement under tension

---

## Behavioral Tracking and Identity Evolution

The system continuously tracks:

- Activity types

- Duration and intensity patterns

- Consistency over time

- Distribution of effort across modalities

This data is not exposed raw to the player.

It is used to:

- Adjust class bias

- Unlock hybrid paths

- Surface new activity variants

---

## Hybrid Class Evolution

When a player consistently trains outside their primary bias:

- Hybrid paths emerge

- Activity pools blend

- Loot tables soften into shared affinities

Examples:

- Strider–Unbroken: explosive endurance

- Forgeborn–Unbroken: power conditioning

- Forgeborn–Adept: controlled strength

- Adept–Strider: efficient, resilient endurance

Hybridization is:

- Gradual

- Behavior-driven

- Never menu-based

---

## What Classes Are Not

Classes are not:

- RPG job locks

- Recovery modes

- Secondary systems

- Static identities

They are paths of endurance, not cages.
