# Endure – Core Gameplay Loop (Locked)

## Design Intent
Endure is a progression-driven fantasy experience where real-world physical effort fuels character growth, loot acquisition, and long-term survival. The system rewards choice, persistence, and consistency, while allowing identity to evolve organically over time.

The core gameplay loop is session-based and repeatable, supported by a persistent meta layer that reinforces long-term engagement without disrupting moment-to-moment progression.

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

### 1. Commit to a Class and Activity
At onboarding, the player selects a primary class based on the type of physical activity they most prefer.

Class selection:
- Establishes initial character identity
- Biases available activities toward that class
- Influences early stat emphasis and loot tables

The player then commits to a specific activity.

Each activity defines:
- Expected effort (time, intensity, or volume)
- Risk tier (standard, elite, boss)
- Guaranteed XP range
- Milestone structure during the activity
- Loot opportunities (milestones and completion)

This step establishes player agency, identity, and stakes before effort begins.

---

### 2. Perform Real-World Effort
The player completes the activity in the real world.

Effort is interpreted using broad, trust-based signals such as:
- Completion
- Duration or volume
- Intensity or difficulty selection
- Consistency over time

Precision tracking is intentionally avoided. Honest engagement is the baseline expectation.

---

### 3. Effort Converts to Guaranteed XP
Upon meeting minimum completion criteria:
- XP is always awarded
- XP scales predictably with effort
- Diminishing returns prevent single-activity exploitation

XP represents character mastery and capability and is never random.

This answers the question:
“Did this session move my character forward?”

The answer is always yes if the player showed up.

---

### 4. Earn Loot and Resources

#### 4a. Milestone Rewards (During Activity)
During longer or more demanding activities, the player crosses effort milestones.

At each milestone:
- A low-probability loot roll occurs
- Loot quality is intentionally modest
- The primary reward is Resources

Resources:
- Are the primary currency of the game
- Fuel the survival and meta-progression layer
- Can also be used for select character needs

More effort or longer sessions result in more milestone opportunities.

---

#### 4b. Completion Loot Roll (End of Activity)
Upon successful completion:
- A full loot roll occurs
- Loot quality and rarity scale with activity difficulty and modifiers
- Rewards focus on gear, affixes, and build-defining items

XP is guaranteed. Loot is chance-based.

---

### 5. Apply Power and Evolve Identity
The player equips, modifies, stores, or discards loot and resources.

Results include:
- Stat changes
- Mechanical modifiers
- Visual identity expression
- Build specialization

The system tracks behavioral patterns across activities.

If a player consistently engages outside their original class bias:
- Hybrid class paths unlock
- New activity variants become available
- Identity evolves based on behavior, not respec menus

Class identity is reinforced by choice, not locked by declaration.

---

### 6. Spend Resources
Resources earned through milestones and activities can be spent in two domains:

#### 6a. Survival / Hold Progression
Resources are used to:
- Build and improve the persistent Hold
- Reduce long-term friction
- Improve recovery and session flow
- Provide resilience against inconsistency

This progression is persistent, slow, forgiving, and never punitive.

#### 6b. Character Utility
Resources may also be used for:
- Gear repair or maintenance
- Cosmetics and visual identity
- Quality-of-life systems

Resources do not replace XP or loot.

---

### 7. Unlock Harder Challenges and Repeat
As character power, class depth, and hybrid options increase:
- Harder activity tiers unlock
- Elite and boss encounters become available
- Loot tables expand
- Modifiers introduce new risk and reward dynamics

The loop then repeats with higher stakes and more meaningful choices.

The player is no longer “working out.”
They are playing Endure.

---

## Separation of Concerns (Non-Negotiable)
- Core Loop: XP, loot, builds, mastery
- Meta Layer: Persistence, resilience, continuity

The two systems support each other but never replace one another.

---

## One-Line Summary
Choose a class-favored activity, perform real-world effort, earn guaranteed XP, gain milestone resources and roll for loot, apply power and evolve identity, invest resources into survival and utility, unlock harder challenges, and repeat.
