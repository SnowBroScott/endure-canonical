## XP – Conceptual Model (Locked)

In Endure, XP represents sustained real-world effort and serves as a reliable ledger of endurance.

XP is not a reward for performance, optimization, or perfection. XP confirms that effort genuinely occurred.

### Earning XP
- XP is awarded only after a meaningful portion of a committed activity is completed.
- This minimum threshold exists to ensure effort is intentional and non-trivial.
- Once the threshold is crossed, XP accrues steadily as the player continues to endure.

### What Influences XP
- XP increases with time endured.
- XP is modestly influenced by the difficulty of the committed activity.
- Stopping early still grants proportional XP based on how much endurance occurred.
- Continuing beyond the prescribed activity grants additional XP.

### Completion
- Completing the activity grants full XP for that commitment.
- Completion recognition (bonuses, rewards) is handled through loot and narrative systems, not XP.
- XP remains a measure of endurance, not accomplishment.

### What XP Does Not Do
- XP does not punish missed days or inactivity.
- XP does not decay or reset.
- XP does not require daily participation.
- XP does not create failure states once effort genuinely begins.

XP exists to build long-term trust that endurance always leads to progress and to pace future transformation systems.
