# **Endure – Resources (Currency System)**

## **Design Intent**

Resources are the residual value of endurance. They are not earned directly — they are what remains when a player chooses not to use a piece of loot. Every resource in a player’s account is the consequence of a decision: something was offered, considered, and converted.

Resources exist to ensure that no loot outcome ever feels wasted. Destroying loot is not failure. It is authorship.

## **How Resources Are Earned**

Resources flow entirely through the loot pipeline. There are no direct resource drops.

- Loot is acquired during activities — at milestones (lower probability of being meaningful) and at completion (higher probability of being meaningful).

- Each piece of loot has two valid outcomes: use it, or destroy it.

- Loot that is destroyed — by choice or through natural dissolution (consumables) — converts into resources.

- Milestone loot trends toward resource conversion because it is intentionally less likely to be meaningful. This is a loot quality distinction, not a separate resource mechanic.

*A player with more resources is a player who has endured a great deal and found most of what was offered not worth keeping. That surplus is a reflection of history, not a problem to solve.*

## **Currency Structure**

Resources are a single unified currency. All modalities produce the same resource through the same pipeline. A Strider and a Forgeborn completing equivalent effort and destroying equivalent loot earn the same resources.

This is non-negotiable. No modality produces a more valuable or more useful currency than another. Resources are equal across all paths of endurance.

## **Storage**

Resources accumulate freely. There is no storage cap. A player who has been consistently converting loot for months should never hit a ceiling. Surplus is honored, not penalized.

## **What Resources Are Spent On**

Resources are spent almost exclusively within the survival/build layer. Sinks are designed to feel like expressions of surplus agency — things a player is glad to have resources for, but never blocked without them.

### **Hold Construction (Primary Sink)**

Resources are spent during the Commitment phase of construction to bring a blueprint into the world. This is the primary and most significant sink in the game. It represents endurance sustaining and shaping the world the player inhabits.

### **Earned Marks (Identity Commemoration)**

Resources may be spent to commit an earned mark — a tattoo, scar, symbol, or ritual trace — to permanent expression on the player’s character.

This is not cosmetic customization. Cosmetic expression (hairstyle, clothing, body presentation) is always free and reversible. Earned marks are different: they are memories given form. The mark itself is earned through experience. Resources are the cost of the ritual that makes it permanent and visible.

*This distinction is foundational to the identity framework:*

- Expression is choice. Always free, always reversible, outcome-neutral.

- Marks are memory. Earned through moments that mattered. Resources are the ritual cost of permanence.

## **What Resources Are Not**

Resources must never touch the core progression layer. This is non-negotiable.

- Resources do not replace XP or loot.

- Resources do not affect activity difficulty.

- Resources do not affect loot probability.

- Resources do not affect transformation speed or hybrid progression.

- Resources do not create failure states or punishment for not spending.

- Gear repair and maintenance are not resource sinks. Resources are not spent on core activity readiness.

## **Relationship to Other Systems**

- Loot System: Resources are the downstream output of loot decisions. Loot design determines resource flow volume. These systems are tightly coupled.

- Construction System: Hold construction is the primary sink. Resource cost occurs at the Commitment phase.

- Identity System: Earned marks are the only non-build resource sink. This is the one point where resources touch the identity layer, and it does so only at the commemorative level — never at the progression level.

- Core Gameplay Loop: Resources are referenced as milestone rewards in the Core Loop document. This is accurate in outcome but not in mechanism — milestone drops are low-probability loot, and the resources they produce are the result of those drops being destroyed, not direct drops.

## **One-Line Summary**

*Resources are what endurance leaves behind when loot is not wanted — a single unified currency that funds the world and commemorates identity, and never touches progression.*
